Plant Care

Plant care involves the necessary actions and practices to ensure the health and well-being of plants. It includes providing appropriate watering, light, temperature, and humidity conditions, as well as regular pruning, fertilization, and protection against pests and diseases.

Seeds Care

Seed care involves the practices necessary to ensure successful germination and healthy growth of seeds. Seed care is crucial in establishing strong and healthy plants from the start, setting the foundation for a successful gardening or farming endeavor.

Root

A root plant, also known as a root vegetable, is a type of plant where the edible part is primarily the root portion. These plants store their energy and nutrients in their underground roots, which grow and develop to various sizes, shapes, and colors.

Stem

Stems are an essential part of plants, serving as the main structural and support system that connects the roots to the leaves and other above-ground parts. They play a crucial role in providing transportation of water, nutrients, and sugars between the roots and the rest of the plant.

Leaves Care

Leaves are the primary photosynthetic organs of plants, typically found attached to the stems or branches. They are essential for the plant's survival as they capture sunlight and convert it into chemical energy through the process of photosynthesis.

Vegetable Care

Vegetables are edible plants or parts of plants that are primarily consumed for their nutritional value and culinary purposes. They are an important part of a healthy diet, providing essential vitamins, minerals, and dietary fiber.
